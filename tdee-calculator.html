This tool uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, a commonly used estimate for basal metabolic rate (BMR), then multiplies by your activity level to estimate TDEE.
What the numbers mean
  • BMR – estimated calories your body uses at rest, all day.
  • TDEE – BMR multiplied by your activity level.

As a rough guide:

  • Weight loss: TDEE − 250 to 500 calories per day.
  • Maintenance: around your TDEE.
  • Weight gain: TDEE + 250 to 500 calories per day.

This calculator is for general informational purposes only and is not medical advice. Talk to a healthcare professional before making significant changes to diet or activity.
