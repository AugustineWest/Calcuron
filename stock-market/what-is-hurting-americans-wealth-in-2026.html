In tight-budget environments, credit cards often become the ‚Äúbridge‚Äù people use to keep up with bills ‚Äî which can backfire if balances grow faster than income.

Healthcare is uniquely damaging to wealth because it can be both predictable (premiums, deductibles) and unpredictable (surprise illness/injury, out-of-network events, chronic conditions).

Wealth damage rarely comes from one dramatic event. It‚Äôs usually a slow leak: higher expenses, higher payments, lower savings, then one surprise bill.

What Can Households Control?

Macro conditions are real ‚Äî but households still have levers:

  • Build an emergency buffer (even a small one changes outcomes).
  • Keep investing consistently (time is the compounding engine).
